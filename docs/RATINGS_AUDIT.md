## 1. The problem (original model)

Ratings are derived from real box-score data, percentile-ranked **within each player's peak decade**,
then mapped to 0-99. The first version had three systemic faults:

1. **Severe OVR compression.** The entire top 100 spanned only ~89 → 81 (an 8-point band) and the
   single highest OVR in the whole database was 89. There was no headroom to separate an all-time
   great from a good starter — Jordan, LeBron and Curry all sat in the mid-80s alongside role players.
2. **Thin-era inflation.** Because ratings were percentile-ranked *within a decade*, a top-of-era
   player in the shallow, stat-inflated 1960s–70s leagues scored as high as a top-of-era star today.
   Versatile old bigs rocketed up — **Alvan Adams ranked #4 all-time**, above Jordan/LeBron/Curry,
   with Rich Kelley, Larry Kenon, Bob Lanier and Mickey Johnson all in the top ~35.
3. **Modern creators suppressed.** Trae Young (75, #583), Ja Morant, SGA, Tatum and Luka all landed
   *below* 1970s role players. Box-score steals/blocks ≠ defense and the height map penalizes guards,
   so perimeter creators got dragged down by DEF / ATH / HGT / REB.

The career-average representative line also diluted the durable greats (Kareem/Kobe/LeBron) with their
long rookie + decline tails, pushing their numbers down.

## 2. What changed (`scripts/data/build.ts`)

1. **Prime-weighted stats.** Token/rookie/decline seasons (< half a player's peak-minutes season) are
   dropped and the biggest seasons are emphasised (minutes^1.3). Ratings now reflect a player's
   *prime*, like 2K — durable greats stop being diluted by long tails.
2. **Star-separating curve.** `rate(p) = clamp(round(36 + 63·p^1.25), 25, 99)`. The ceiling rose
   95 → 99 (so all-time greats can reach the top of the scale) and the convex shape (exponent > 1)
   makes the elite pull away from the merely-good instead of bunching in the 80s.
3. **Era-strength discount** (skill ratings only — **not** height or durability): a points haircut by
   peak decade — `1940s −6, 1950s −4.5, 1960s −3, 1970s −4, 1980s −1.5, 1990s −0.5, 2000s+ 0`.
   Percentile-within-decade otherwise treats a shallow old league as equal to today's far deeper one;
   true greats sit high enough in their era to survive the haircut, role players fall to where they
   belong. Height (a 7-footer is 7 feet in any era) and durability (era-neutral longevity) are exempt.

**Effect:** top-50 is now spread across every decade (≈ 2010s 13, 2000s 10, 1990s 7, 1980s 5, 1970s 6,
1960s 4, 2020s 5). Alvan Adams fell #4 → #22. Signature traits pop correctly — Curry 97 SHO / 98 CLU,
Magic 98 PLY, Wilt 96 ATH / 94 REB, Westbrook & Luka 99 PLY, Reggie 95 SHO, Gobert 98 DEF.

## 3. Known remaining off-cases (candidates for a 2K override pass)

These are the limits of a box-score model under the **locked OVR weights** — best fixed with a curated
2K-derived override list rather than more formula tuning:

- **Karl-Anthony Towns too high (≈ #3).** The defense proxy (steals + blocks + rebound %) and the
  clutch proxy (points + TS% + usage) both over-reward high-usage stretch bigs. Same effect inflates
  Embiid's and Gobert's DEF.
- **Non-shooting bigs a touch low** — Wilt, Kareem, Shaq, Duncan. The .20 shooting weight (built from
  FT% / 3P% / efficiency) punishes career poor free-throw shooters even when they were dominant.
- **Bill Russell broken (≈ #1261).** His entire value was defense, rebounding and winning; pre-1974
  there are no steals/blocks, his scoring/FT% were low, and rings aren't in the box score. Pure
  override case.
- **Trae Young / small score-first guards low.** With the locked weights, four categories (DEF, REB,
  HGT, ATH) at 55-65 cap a defensively-limited 6'1" guard around 73-78, regardless of elite
  shooting/playmaking. 2K rates these players higher via position-relative weighting.

## 4. Suggestions

- **2K override list (highest leverage).** Provide name → 9 category values for the marquee tier
  (top ~100 + current stars). Layered last in `data-source/curated/overrides.json`, absolute values
  bypass the formula and curve entirely. Start with the off-cases in §3.
- **Athleticism & Clutch are the weakest proxies** (box stats barely capture them). 2K's
  speed/vertical/clutch attributes would be the most valuable external data to import here.
- **Defense** is similarly limited — steals + blocks over-reward bigs and miss perimeter stoppers; a
  2K defense/perimeter-D value would help most.
- **Consider position-relative OVR weighting** (long-term, if the locked weights are ever revisited):
  weighting playmaking/shooting more for guards and rebounding/height more for bigs would lift
  score-first guards (Trae, Booker) without distorting bigs — closest to how 2K computes overall.
- **Re-tune the era discount** if the 2K list shifts the balance; it's a single `ERA_PEN` table in
  `build.ts`.
